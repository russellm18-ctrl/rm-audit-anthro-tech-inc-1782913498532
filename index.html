// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your case study images—the WaTech persona work, the oncology UX research, the WCAG compliance project—are almost certainly sitting on your server as unlabeled decorative files with no JSON-LD schema connecting them to the service pages they support, no descriptive alt-text, and stripped EXIF data that tells search engines nothing. A procurement officer at a federal agency right now is searching "human-centered design consultancy GSA MAS contract" and your competitors are capturing that click because their case study assets are structured as indexed content objects, not decoration. Your GSA MAS contract announcement is a high-authority credentialing signal that should be anchored in schema markup and image metadata—and it isn't doing that work.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: The government agency program director evaluating your WCAG compliance services needs to see your team in the room—facilitators running usability sessions, researchers engaging with disability communities, whiteboards from the WaTech 10-agency alignment project. What you're showing instead are text blocks making claims about rigor and collaboration that have zero visual corroboration, which to a cautious public-sector buyer reads as a vendor with something to hide. Government procurement decisions are risk-mitigation decisions, and your site gives them no photographic evidence of process, people, or scale.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: A sharp competitor in the civic UX space—think firms like Coforma or Bixal—publishes documented visual process libraries: workshop photography, annotated wireframe walkthroughs, and team-in-context imagery tied directly to federal deliverables. You have a ClearMark Award win for Washington State's unemployment benefits page redesign and a cancer care equity project—two extraordinarily compelling, visually rich stories—and neither one contains a single photograph of the humans who did the work or the sessions where it happened. That absence hands a $200K–$500K government contract renewal conversation to whoever shows up with a deck that has faces, rooms, and process in it.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a structured operational photography deployment built around your active service lines—usability testing sessions, accessibility evaluations, client co-design workshops—and embed every captured asset with a strict metadata protocol: descriptive alt-text referencing the specific service (e.g., "usability testing session for government digital service accessibility evaluation"), JSON-LD schema linking images to corresponding case study and service pages, and geo and organizational metadata where applicable. Attach this protocol to your existing GSA MAS contract content so federal search queries surface indexed, schema-reinforced visual proof rather than a text wall. This converts your photography from a cosmetic afterthought into a structured data layer that actively routes high-intent buyers from search directly to credentialed proof of delivery.
